Méthode Champenoise

How the bubbles are born.

I

Second fermentation

A little yeast and sugar are added, and the wine ferments a second time — sealed inside the bottle, where the bubbles are trapped.

II

Aging on the lees

The wine rests on its spent yeast for years, drawing out brioche, almond and that unmistakable depth.

III

Riddling

Each bottle is gradually turned and tilted, coaxing the sediment down into the neck.

IV

Disgorgement

The neck is frozen and the plug of sediment fired out — leaving the wine perfectly clear.

V

Dosage

A final touch of wine and sugar sets the sweetness — from bone-dry Brut Nature to richer styles.
